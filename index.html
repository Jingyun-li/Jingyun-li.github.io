Beat 913 past year limited longitude¶

This dataset records all crimes that happened from 10/14/2012 to 10/13/2013 in Bridgeport, Chicago, IL. The dataset records a lot of features of the data. Among all these, some important ones are the case number, type of crime, description, if the criminal got arrested or not, is the crime domestic or not, longitude, and latitude.

The dataset was gathered from https://data.cityofchicago.org/Public-Safety/Beat-913-past-year-limited-longitude/idpc-fjqg/data

I then divided all the data into four groups, and marked them with red, orange, green, and blue:

  1. domestic crime with criminal ultimately arrested
  2. non-domestic crime with criminal ultimately arrested
  3. domestic crime with criminal ultimately not arrested

for index, curr in df.iterrows():
    if curr["Arrest"] and curr["Domestic"]:
        folium.Marker(location=[curr["Latitude"], curr["Longitude"]],
                    icon=folium.Icon(color='red')).add_to(map_osm)
    elif curr["Arrest"] and not curr["Domestic"]:
        folium.Marker(location=[curr["Latitude"], curr["Longitude"]],
                    icon=folium.Icon(color='orange')).add_to(map_osm)
    elif not curr["Arrest"] and curr["Domestic"]:
        folium.Marker(location=[curr["Latitude"], curr["Longitude"]],
                    icon=folium.Icon(color='green')).add_to(map_osm)    
    else:
        folium.Marker(location=[curr["Latitude"], curr["Longitude"]],
            icon=folium.Icon(color='blue')).add_to(map_osm)

Most of the pinpoints on the map were blue, meaning that most crimes that happened in between that time in this specific area tended to be non-domestic with criminals ultimately not arrested. Green and yellow pinpoints are not scattered around as much, but they still took up a large portion of the pain points. This means non-domestic crimes with criminals ultimately arrested and domestic crimes with criminals ultimately not arrested are somewhat rare compared to non-domestic crimes with criminals ultimately not arrested. Red pinpoints take up the smallest proportion, meaning that domestic crime with criminals ultimately arrested is the most uncommon crime of all that is listed here. it is also interesting to know that the red pinpoints seem to be gathering in the upper part of the map rather than their counterparts with no obvious pattern.
