Health Insurance Costs

Impact of Demographics and Lifestyle on Health Insurance Premiums

This project explores how demographic and lifestyle factors — such as age, BMI, smoking habits, and region — influence the cost of health insurance premiums. Using regression models and data visualization, we identified key patterns that highlight the significance of preventive health and demographic targeting in pricing models.

Data & Methodology

The dataset contained records of individuals with attributes including age, gender, BMI, region, number of children, and smoking status. The analysis was conducted in Python using Pandas, Matplotlib, and scikit-learn to build predictive models and visualize the cost relationships.

  • Used multiple linear regression to model premium costs.
  • Visualized trends between BMI and charges using scatter plots and regression lines.
  • Compared average costs across demographic categories (e.g., smoker vs. non-smoker).

Findings

Smoking status had the strongest impact on premiums, increasing costs by over 200% on average. BMI and age also showed a positive correlation with charges, while gender and region had limited effects. The project concludes that predictive pricing models could be improved with behavioral data and preventive metrics.
