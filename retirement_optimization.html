Retirement Portfolio Optimization

Dynamic programming and simulation-driven investment strategy modeling.

View Full Project

This project develops a quantitative model to generate personalized retirement investment advice for clients with varying income growth, age, and savings behavior. The model identifies the optimal portfolio allocation strategy that maximizes lifetime expected utility under uncertainty, using tools such as stochastic dynamic programming and Monte Carlo simulation.

Methodology

The optimization framework combines dynamic programming with stochastic market simulations to determine an optimal year-by-year allocation strategy. The utility function penalizes shortfalls below retirement targets, balancing risk and reward over time.

  • Dynamic Policy Modeling: Each simulated path evaluates annual portfolio choices based on wealth, age, and expected return.
  • Monte Carlo Simulation: Thousands of correlated market outcomes are generated to approximate real-world uncertainty.
  • Utility Optimization: The objective maximizes expected lifetime utility while imposing downside risk constraints.

Findings

Simulation results show that investment strategies evolve naturally with age, wealth, and proximity to retirement. Younger clients allocate more toward growth-oriented portfolios, while older investors transition toward capital preservation. Clients exceeding their savings trajectory maintain stability, whereas those behind their targets adopt more aggressive allocations to catch up.

  • Wealth Sensitivity: Higher wealth correlates with reduced risk-taking and lower fund volatility.
  • Age Dependency: Younger investors prefer equities and higher-volatility portfolios; older ones favor bonds and stability.
  • Model Resolution: Finer grid sizes and larger scenario counts yield smoother, more stable policy surfaces.

Key Insights

This study demonstrates how quantitative methods can guide practical financial planning. The integrated approach of stochastic dynamic programming and Monte Carlo simulation yields interpretable, data-driven investment strategies that align with modern portfolio theory and lifecycle investing principles. These models empower financial advisors and clients alike to make adaptive, informed decisions in uncertain markets.
